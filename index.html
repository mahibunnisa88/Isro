New missions planned for 2026


Gaganyaan Mission

India's first manned space mission, planned for 2026, will send astronauts into Low Earth Orbit
using ISRO‚Äôs GSLV Mk III rocket.This mission aims to establish India‚Äôs independent human
spaceflight capability, paving the way for future interplanetary missions and space tourism


Samudrayaan Mission

Also set for 2026, this mission will deploy a three-member crew in a submersible to a depth of
6,000 meters to study marine biodiversity and seabed mineral resources. Developed by the
National Institute of Ocean Technology, it supports India‚Äôs Blue Economy


Chandrayaan-4 Mission

Planned for 2027 but preparations will intensify in 2026, Chandrayaan-4 will be India‚Äôs first lunar
sample return mission. It involves multiple launches and orbital assembly of modules for collecting
and returning Moon soil and rock to Earth, potentially with international partners


Multiple Satellite Launches

ISRO plans to launch at least nine more missions before March 2026, including commercial communication
satellites like BlueBird Block-2, navigation satellites such as NVS-03 on GSLV, user-funded satellites
on PSLV, and upcoming SSLV missions promoting private industry involvement.
