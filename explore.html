Satish Dhawan Space Centre (SHAR) at Sriharikota, 80 km from Chennai, is India‚Äôs main spaceport with two active launch pads ‚Äì FLP and SLP.

It began in 1971 with a sounding rocket and now has world-class facilities for solid motor production, propellant servicing, testing, and housing for staff and visitors.

FLP (First Launch Pad), built in the 1990s, uses the ‚ÄúIntegrate on Pad‚Äù concept with a 3,200-ton Mobile Service Tower; active since 1993 with 37 launches.

Department of Space (DOS) is Government of India department mandated with the execution of Indian Space Programme. The Department of Space has evolved the following programmes with the objective of promoting & developing application of space science and space technology: Launch Vehicle programme having indigenous capability for launching satellites.INSAT Programme for telecommunications, broadcasting, meteorology, development of education etc. Remote Sensing Programme for application of satellite imagery for various developmental purposes.Research and Development in Space Sciences and Technology for serving the end of applying them for national development. Achievements


Pioneering Launch Vehicles: Developed SLV, ASLV, PSLV, GSLV, and LVM3 for launching satellites into various orbits.


Satellite Programs: Successfully launched satellites for communication, Earth observation, navigation (NavIC), and scientific missions.

Chandrayaan-1 (2008): First Indian lunar probe, discovered water on the Moon.

Mangalyaan / Mars Orbiter Mission (2013): First Indian Mars mission, reaching orbit on first attempt.

Chandrayaan-2 (2019): Advanced lunar mission with orbiter, lander, and rover.

Chandrayaan-3 (2023): Successfully achieved soft landing on the Moon‚Äôs South Pole, making India the first Asian country to do so.

Astrosat (2015): India‚Äôs first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory.

International Collaboration: Launched 319 foreign satellites from 33 countries via PSLV.

Navigation & Augmentation: Developed NavIC (regional navigation system) and GAGAN for civil aviation.

Education & Outreach: Established IIST, IIRS, and programs like YUVIKA and Space Tutor for training future scientists.

Technological Innovations: Indigenous cryogenic stages, reusable launch vehicle technology, small satellite launchers,
and human-rated vehicles for Gaganyaan.
